## Design Context

### Users
Home lab operators and small DevOps teams who run self-hosted Docker Compose stacks.
They check this dashboard when something is wrong, or when they want to confirm
everything is running. They are technical, busy, and impatient. They don't want to
explore — they want to know immediately. Primary job: "Is everything up? What broke?
Fix it now."

### Brand Personality
**Sharp. Technical. Precise.**

Emotional goal: **Power** — the user should feel in command of their infrastructure.
Not reassured, not delighted — *in control*. Like an operator with a clear readout.

Anti-references: crowded, bloated interfaces. Consumer apps, pastel dashboards, "friendly"
SaaS UIs, anything with rounded-corner card-heavy layouts that feels like a Notion template.

References: **GitHub** (information density, dark palette, precise typography, restraint
with colour) and **Grafana** (structured data panels, status at a glance, purposeful
colour use). Not the visual style wholesale — the *discipline* behind them.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark only.** Background `#0d1117` (GitHub-dark base). No light mode.
- **Accent colour: electric indigo `#6c63ff`** — distinct from the status colours
  (green/red/yellow/blue are all taken by health states), indigo reads as "action/brand"
  without competing. Pairs with the blue→green logo gradient.
- **Typography: monospace for data, sans-serif for labels.**
  Use `JetBrains Mono` for SHAs, container IDs, ports, env keys, logs.
  Use `DM Sans` for all UI labels and body text. `Inter` is banned.
  Keep base size 13–14px. Dense but not cramped.
- **Zero decoration.** No gradients on UI chrome, no drop shadows on cards, no
  rounded corners above 6px. Borders are 1px solid, never glowing.
- **Status colours are sacred.** Never repurpose green/red/yellow/blue for anything
  other than health states. These must be instantly readable at a glance.
- **Motion: minimal and functional.** Only two animations are allowed:
  1. Sync button spin (communicates active work)
  2. Detail panel fade-in (14px translateY, 150ms ease-out — prevents jarring cut)
  All other animations are off by default. Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — every pixel must carry information or create structure.
   White space is used for separation, not aesthetics.
2. **Status first** — health indicators (running/stopped/degraded/error) must be
   immediately visible without reading text. Colour + shape, not colour alone.
3. **Hierarchy is the UX** — Repo → Stack → Container is the core mental model.
   The UI must make this hierarchy obvious at every level.
4. **Actions are explicit** — buttons have clear labels or aria-labels. Nothing is
   hidden behind hover states. Force sync is always one click away.
5. **Fail loudly, recover gracefully** — errors are shown immediately with a clear
   next action. Never silent failures. Never broken empty states.
6. **Silent by default** — the UI should not animate, alert, or label gratuitously.
   Every interaction should feel immediate and intentional.
